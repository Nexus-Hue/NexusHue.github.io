The characters accompanying Santa Catrina out of the underworld are inspired by the Cora people. Los Cora, are indigenous to the Sierra del Nayar in Nayarit, Mexico, and have distinct traditional clothing that reflects their cultural heritage

  • Shirts and Pants: Men typically wear simple, white cotton shirts and pants. The shirts are often long-sleeved and can be embroidered with colorful patterns.
  • Headgear: A traditional straw hat, sometimes decorated with ribbons or embroidery, is commonly worn.
  • Blouses: Women wear blouses made from cotton, usually white, and often embroidered with vibrant, geometric patterns around the neck and sleeves.

The set design is inspired by Maya temples. These temples often feature statues of Quetzalcoatl and Tlaloc. These deities held significant roles in Maya religion and cosmology.
